# 4 Lessons I learnt till 18

Before we begin, yes, 4 has no correlation to 18 but I have yet more to live so that is a decent amount of lessons to learn. Quite recently I have felt a change in myself. It is a very subtle shift, but as the intuitive person I like to think I am, this change of flow has led me to ponder how I have lived through the 18 revolutions this Earth has taken since I was born.

Here are some of the things I’ve come to believe, things that I feel are some facts every individual should keep in mind in order to make the best in life. These ‘lessons’ have been derived from personal experiences in my life which have really impacted me and have always pushed me to take a broader view about how I perceive myself.

**1) You are the main character of your life**

This thing might sound obvious but your life is only your life. So do all things that YOU want to do. If you want to go to take your own picture at the top of a summit while everyone else wants to go back, stop and take that picture. You don’t need to hurry to stay with everyone else. You make your own memories based on how happy and content you felt in that moment. Never let people stop you from doing the things you love because you ARE the main character in this life. It is never that deep because those who don’t add to your growth and belittle you for having hobbies they don’t have, are never meant to stay with you for life anyways. It is your movie so live like you own it!

**2) Be who you would be proud of when you die**

Many times we are faced with decisions which force us to choose between what is ‘right’ for us and what we want. The more career oriented job versus something we are really passionate about. Whatever decision you make, at the end when you are 90 years old, you should be proud of what you have accomplished in your life. While that money-minting job is important, it doesn’t mean you throw those painting skills out of the window. Life is all about experiences. The more memorable, the better. The more money, the more options to choose from. These experiences help you grow as a person and catalyze changes which make you worldlier.

**3) Self discipline is not a choice**

If you are on a quest like me, to be better than oneself then it is very important to be strict with yourself. If you want to have an aura that attracts, that is worthy of being listened to then it is important for you to be someone you yourself are attracted to, someone you yourself want to follow. Self discipline is not a choice in this case, it is more like a plank on a wooden ladder without which you can’t climb higher. So if your morning 7 am to 8 am timeslot is set for exercise then don’t let yourself make excuses for that one morning when it is too cold and staying in seems like a better option. Because once you allow yourself that momentary bliss of not putting in the hard work and determination, your mind will be addicted to it.

**4) It is ok to feel lost sometimes**

Coming to this last point, while I may talk about choosing oneself or being happy in life, it is ok to feel lost sometimes. To not know what is right or wrong or even possible. What always works for me is to take a step back and stop thinking. Just exist in this limbo for a while and try to connect with the nature or people around you. You don’t need to have all the answers or know what you will do. You are your best support and trust that!

To conclude, while this blog might sound rather Gen-Z like, these realizations I am trying to share all stem from me understanding my self worth and working on making myself someone my younger self would be proud of. A lot of people just need support and understanding, and if reading this makes you or someone else feel less guilty, then this’ll be worth it.

Until next time, friends. If this resonated with you, you know what to do!
